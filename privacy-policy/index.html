1. Introduction

Welcome to W4NN4D13 Security Research ("we," "us," "our," or "the Website"), accessible at https://w4nn4d13.tech. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard information when you visit our website.

W4NN4D13 Security Research is an independent cybersecurity research platform dedicated to the publication of:

  • Vulnerability disclosures and responsible disclosure reports
  • Bug bounty write-ups detailing findings submitted to public and private programs
  • Digital Forensics and Incident Response (DFIR) articles and case studies
  • Offensive and defensive security research, including proof-of-concept demonstrations
  • Educational content covering web application security, network security, reverse engineering, and penetration testing techniques
  • Security analysis of publicly disclosed CVEs, malware campaigns, and threat actor TTPs

We are committed to protecting your privacy. This policy is intended to be transparent about our data practices. By accessing or using this website, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy.

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties. We do not intentionally collect sensitive personal data such as financial information, government-issued identification, health records, or passwords.

\2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information Collected Automatically

When you visit W4NN4D13 Security Research, certain technical information may be collected automatically through our infrastructure, analytics services, and content delivery networks. This information is used in aggregate or pseudonymized form and may include:

  • IP Address: Your public-facing IP address, which may be used for security, fraud prevention, geographic analytics, and traffic routing. IP addresses may be truncated or anonymized depending on the service configuration.
  • Browser Information: Browser type and version (e.g., Chrome 124, Firefox 126), rendering engine, and browser language preferences.
  • Device Information: Operating system and version, device type (desktop, mobile, tablet), screen resolution, and hardware model where inferable from the user agent string.
  • Pages Visited: URLs of pages accessed on our website, including query strings where present.
  • Referrer URL: The URL of the page or search engine from which you arrived at our website.
  • Timestamps: Date and time of your visit, session duration, and time spent on individual pages.
  • Interaction Data: Scroll depth, click events, and navigation patterns as captured by analytics tools.
  • Network Information: HTTP request headers, connection type, and approximate geographic location derived from IP address (typically at the city or country level).

2.2 Information You Voluntarily Provide

Our website is primarily a read-only research publication platform. We do not operate user registration, login systems, or e-commerce. However, if you contact us via our contact page or any linked communication channel, you may voluntarily provide:

  • Your name or pseudonym
  • Your email address
  • The content of your message or inquiry

Any information submitted through contact forms is used solely to respond to your inquiry and is not added to any marketing list.

2.3 Information We Do NOT Collect

We do not intentionally collect the following:

  • Social security numbers, national ID numbers, or government-issued identification
  • Financial information, credit card numbers, or banking details
  • Health or medical information
  • Biometric data
  • Passwords or authentication credentials
  • Precise GPS-level location data

4. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

4.1 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by a website you visit. They are widely used to make websites function correctly, improve user experience, and provide website operators with analytical information.

4.2 Types of Cookies We Use

Functional Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the basic operation of the website. They enable core functionality such as page rendering preferences, security tokens, and session continuity. Functional cookies do not track you across other websites and are not used for advertising.

Analytics Cookies

These cookies are placed by Google Analytics to collect anonymized information about how visitors use our website. They help us understand traffic sources, session durations, bounce rates, and content performance. Analytics cookies do not personally identify you.

Security Cookies

These cookies are set by Cloudflare as part of its security and bot management services. They help distinguish legitimate human visitors from automated bots, prevent DDoS attacks, and enforce rate limiting. Cloudflare security cookies are essential to the safe and stable operation of this website.

4.3 Cookie Consent and Management

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the right to consent to or reject non-essential cookies. You can manage your cookie preferences through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View and delete existing cookies
  • Block cookies from specific websites
  • Block third-party cookies entirely
  • Set preferences to be notified when a cookie is set

Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality or performance of this website. Instructions for managing cookies in common browsers:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions

5. Google Analytics

W4NN4D13 Security Research uses Google Analytics, a web analytics service provided by Google LLC ("Google"), to collect and analyze anonymous visitor data.

5.1 What Google Analytics Collects

Google Analytics collects information including pages visited, session duration, geographic location (at the city/country level), device type, browser, and traffic source. This data is processed by Google on our behalf.

5.2 IP Anonymization

We have configured Google Analytics with IP anonymization enabled. This means your full IP address is truncated before being stored or processed by Google, reducing the risk of individual identification.

5.3 Data Sharing

Data collected by Google Analytics is processed in accordance with Google's Privacy Policy, available at https://policies.google.com/privacy. Google may process this data on servers located in the United States or other countries.

5.4 Opting Out of Google Analytics

You may opt out of Google Analytics tracking at any time by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on, available at https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout. Alternatively, you may use privacy-focused browsers or extensions that block analytics trackers.

6. Cloudflare Services

This website is served through Cloudflare, Inc. ("Cloudflare"), a network infrastructure and security company. Cloudflare provides the following services for this website:

  • Content Delivery Network (CDN): Caching and serving static content from edge locations geographically close to visitors to improve page load performance.
  • DDoS Protection: Absorbing and mitigating volumetric and application-layer distributed denial-of-service attacks.
  • Web Application Firewall (WAF): Filtering malicious HTTP requests, including attempts to exploit known web vulnerabilities such as SQL injection, XSS, and path traversal.
  • Bot Management: Identifying and blocking malicious automated traffic while allowing legitimate crawlers and visitors.
  • SSL/TLS Termination: Encrypting traffic between visitors and the Cloudflare edge, and between the edge and our origin server.
  • Traffic Routing: Optimizing the routing of requests to our origin infrastructure.

6.1 Data Processed by Cloudflare

As a reverse proxy and CDN, Cloudflare intercepts all HTTP/S requests to this website. As a result, Cloudflare processes technical metadata including IP addresses, request headers, and response codes. Cloudflare's processing is governed by its own Privacy Policy, available at https://www.cloudflare.com/privacypolicy/.

Cloudflare may set cookies (such as __cf_bm and _cfuvid) on your device for bot detection and session management purposes. These are classified as security cookies and are essential to website protection.

7. Data Retention

We retain automatically collected data for limited periods appropriate to their purpose:

  • Google Analytics data is retained for 26 months by default, as configured in Google Analytics settings, after which it is automatically deleted.
  • Cloudflare logs (including request logs and security event logs) are retained for periods defined by Cloudflare's infrastructure policies, typically between 24 hours and 30 days depending on the log type and plan level.
  • Contact form submissions are retained only as long as necessary to address the inquiry, and are deleted thereafter unless there is a legitimate ongoing reason for retention (e.g., a responsible disclosure coordination process).

We do not maintain long-term archives of individually identifiable visitor data.

8. Data Security

We take reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect the information processed through this website. These include:

  • HTTPS/TLS encryption enforced for all connections to this website via Cloudflare SSL/TLS
  • Web Application Firewall (WAF) filtering malicious requests at the network edge
  • DDoS mitigation to maintain availability and resist infrastructure-level attacks
  • Access controls limiting administrative access to website infrastructure
  • Regular security reviews of third-party integrations and dependencies

However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is completely secure. We cannot guarantee the absolute security of information transmitted to or through our website. In the event of a data breach involving personal information, we will notify affected individuals and relevant authorities as required by applicable law.

10. GDPR Rights (EEA Visitors)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you have the following rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and applicable national implementations:

Right of Access (Article 15)

You have the right to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you and information about how it is processed.

Right to Rectification (Article 16)

You have the right to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data.

Right to Erasure (Article 17)

You have the right to request deletion of your personal data where there is no compelling reason for its continued processing ("right to be forgotten").

Right to Restriction of Processing (Article 18)

You have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances.

Right to Data Portability (Article 20)

Where processing is based on consent or contract and carried out by automated means, you have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format.

Right to Object (Article 21)

You have the right to object to processing of your personal data based on legitimate interests, including profiling.

Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making (Article 22)

You have the right not to be subject to decisions based solely on automated processing, including profiling, that produce legal or similarly significant effects.

Right to Withdraw Consent

Where processing is based on your consent, you may withdraw that consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing prior to withdrawal.

How to Exercise Your Rights

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your national data protection authority (DPA) if you believe your rights have been infringed.
